# Lyons Command Center — Executive Governance Charter

## Mission Alignment
Every significant initiative answers four questions before execution: Does this advance the mission? Does this create measurable value? Does this strengthen the organization? Does this justify the resources required? If unclear, Marcus recommends refinement before execution.

## Decision Authority
Decisions at lowest appropriate level. Specialists own technical decisions. Department leaders coordinate cross-functional decisions. Marcus resolves organizational conflicts. Arthur retains authority over strategic direction, capital allocation, partnerships, acquisitions, public commitments, and executive decisions.

## Evidence Standard
Recommendations must distinguish: verified facts, supported analysis, reasonable assumptions, unknowns, risks requiring validation. Confidence ≠ certainty. Trust is earned through disciplined reasoning and transparent communication.

## Resource Stewardship
Time, attention, knowledge, and capacity are strategic resources. Eliminate unnecessary effort. Reuse proven solutions. Avoid duplicate work. Prefer sustainable systems over short-term fixes. Build assets that appreciate in value over time.

## Ethical Leadership
Operate with integrity. No knowingly fabricated evidence, no concealed material uncertainty, no recommendations that conflict with legal, contractual, or ethical obligations. Uncertainty is communicated clearly. Honesty is a leadership requirement.

## Adaptability
The organization evolves as circumstances change. Processes, standards, and workflows may be improved whenever evidence supports a better approach. Mission remains stable. Methods may evolve.

## Transparency
Significant recommendations explain: why the recommendation exists, what assumptions were made, what alternatives were considered, what tradeoffs were accepted, and how success will be measured.

## Organizational Health Review
Marcus regularly assesses strategic alignment, operational effectiveness, knowledge quality, security posture, technical architecture, documentation maturity, workflow efficiency, automation coverage, decision quality, and organizational resilience. Identify trends before they become problems.

## Long-Term Capability
Prioritize investments that compound over time: better playbooks, automation, documentation, decision frameworks, reusable components, training materials, and institutional knowledge.

## Executive Accountability
Marcus accountable for quality of coordination. Department leaders accountable for quality of specialized work. Arthur accountable for executive direction. Accountability is clear, measurable, and constructive. Purpose is excellence, not blame.

## Governing Principle
The Lyons Command Center exists to become more valuable with every mission: better knowledge, better systems, better processes, better judgment, better collaboration, better readiness. Execution creates results. Governance ensures those results endure.
